What's different
number from number.

A number is a sign for writing a number.
There are many numbers - Arabic, Roman,
Hexadecimal, etc. A number is one of the main
objects of mathematics, which is used
for counting, measuring and labeling.
A number consists of digits. A digit
can be a number, but a number
is not a digit. The number is only 10 (1, 2, 3,
4, 5, 6, 7, 8, 9, 0). These numbers can
be numbers. But for example
some two-digit, three-digit
number cannot be a digit.

Mining and the mining boom.

Mining is the activity of maintaining a distributed
platform and creating new blocks with the ability to
receive a reward in the form of issued currency in
various cryptocurrencies, in particular Bitcoins.
The essence of mining is to select such a value
Nonce(A random number used during
cryptographic communication only once. Nonce is often
a random or pseudo-random number generated
by the authentication protocol), which will allow get a
hash containing a specified number of zeros at the
beginning of this hash. The number of
zeros in the hash is chosen in such a way that each
block appears approximately
once every 6-10 minutes.

A mining boom is when cryptocurrency starts
to rise sharply in price, and because of this,
miners start to buy video cards (because cryptocurrency
mining takes place thanks to them). And there is a shortage
of video cards. The first boom took place in 2017,
when Bitcoin rose to $20,000. But it lasted until 18 years ago,
when it was halved this year. And already at
the beginning of the 21st year, the biggest surge took place.
Bitcoin rose in price to 64,000 dollars.

And this was followed by a shortage of video cards.
Do you want to assemble a PC from the most
modern components? Let be happy. The industry has
struggled with component shortages for years,
but the problem became apparent in 2020
when the pandemic delayed supplies as people
returned to home offices and began buying
the equipment they needed to support their businesses
afloat. Nvidia will also produce special
Cryptocurrency Mining Processors (CMPs), which are not
as complex as conventional graphics cards.

The graphics card manufacturer follows this strategy to
avoid past experiences with the cryptocurrency
market cycle. It will be interesting to see how NVIDIA and
AMD react. Obviously there is more demand than supply, so
it must be very tempting to expand their production lines
and sell many more GPUs at
inflated prices prices - they will not want to miss this
opportunity to make a huge profit. But there is a
real risk that Ethereum could suddenly
collapse and mining it is no longer worth the cost of
electricity.

Then NVIDIA/AMD will be stuck with a load of unsold
GPUs, plus a load of useless manufacturing
capacity, plus a load of useless components; and
Internet stores will be filled with cheap
used graphics processors from miners, which
will fully satisfy the remaining demand from
gamers; and current generation GPUs will
be obsolete in a year or two when the next
generation comes out.
As happened at the end of 2022.
